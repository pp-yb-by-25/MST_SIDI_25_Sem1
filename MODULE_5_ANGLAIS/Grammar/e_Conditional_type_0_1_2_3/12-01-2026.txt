1. Grammar Exercise: Conditional Types 0 and 1
Concepts to Remember:
• Type 0 (General Truths): Used for scientific facts or things that always happen under certain conditions
. The form is: If + simple present, + simple present
.
• Type 1 (Real/Possible Situations): Used for situations that are very likely to happen in the future if a condition is met
. The form is: If + simple present, + simple future (will + verb)
.
Exercise A: General Truths (Type 0) Complete the following sentences using the correct form of the verbs in parentheses
:
1. If you (heat) heat ice, it (melt) melts.
2. If the temperature (drop) drops below zero, water (freeze) freezes.
3. Their baby girl rarely (cry) cries unless she (be) is hungry.
4. If you (press) press this button, the computer (turn on) turns on.
5. If you (heat) heat water, it (boil) boils.
Exercise B: Real/Possible Situations (Type 1) Complete the following sentences using the correct form of the verbs in parentheses
:
1. If you (send) send this letter now, she (receive) will receive  it tomorrow.
2. If I (find) find your ring, I (give) will give it back to you.
3. Peggy (go) will go shopping if she (have)  has time in the afternoon.
4. If they (study / not) don't study harder, they (pass / not) won't pass the exam.
5. You (be able / not) won't be able to sleep if you (watch) watch this scary film.
